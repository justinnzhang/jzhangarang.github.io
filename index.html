My Story

Iâ€™ve always loved creating things. From piano to art classes, I experimented a lot when I was younger. In high school, I finally discovered two things that would come to define who I am today: videography and programming.

Videography

My introduction to making videos came when I was around 11 years old. While playing my favourite games, I would record my game play and upload these videos to YouTube. At the time, I never realized why I loved doing it that much, usually accrediting it to my love for the games. In high school, I found an old DSLR camera in our basement, which led me to creating all sorts of content. Vlogs, school projects, and short films made up a majority of my last two years in high school.

Once I came to university, I was determined to continue the development of my craft, opting to join as many clubs and organizations that I could. Along the way, I was able to learn new skills such as 3D rendering, compositing, and digital design. Today, I am excited to continue my journey and explore a field that I am passionate about.

Programming

My father came from a background in technology. He was able to leave a poor rural village in China and use his education as a tool to eventually come to Canada. His journey inspired me and left a fascination with technology ever since I was a small child. In middle school, I explored simplified programming applications such as Scratch and made simple applications for fun. At home, I would ask him about the new technologies that heâ€™d be working on. High school brought new practical knowledge in coding and an exposure to a brand-new topic, business.

This life-long interest led to my application to Western University for Computer Science and Business. Today, I am continuing to develop my programming skills on practical projects and coursework while also seeking internship experiences to further build my abilities. In the end, I hope to combine both my technical knowledge and business knowledge to become a product manager and become an effective problem solver in todays digital world.
